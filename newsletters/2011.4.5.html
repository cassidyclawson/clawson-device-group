Ben's Letter:

Time for leaner, meaner startups

Friends and colleagues,


The recession ended nearly 18 months ago, yet medical device startups continue to face major difficulties. Funds are scarce, milestones more ambitious and risk tolerance is next to nil. Developing a new medical device has never been more challenging.


This new economic reality hits close to home. I had no choice but to close my company, BC Tech, as a result of the recession. Our "full service" model, which featured integrated

development, manufacturing and regulatory services, became untenable in an environment of reduced budgets and risk aversion.


While outsourcing development with a full service provider like BC Tech was a luxurious experience for our clients, I'm doubtful that the model will recover. Luxury in business is a thing of the past.


So how does a medical device startup succeed in 2011? Successful startups secure the best talent while keeping overheads low. Many now use the "virtual model" to reduce fixed costs: no offices, no equipment and no salaried employees. Project management is more important than ever – all work must be performed with strict oversight and based on continuous risk assessment and reduction. Personal connections are extremely valuable. To achieve ambitious goals with a limited number of players, startups need preferential access to a broad network of reliable suppliers and specialized contributors


I founded Clawson Device Group to help startups develop new products in this tough new business environment. Together we can achieve progress at breakneck speed without breaking the bank.


If you're an entrepreneur or member of a medical device startup, we should talk.
